Toolkit for Media Literacy

Step 1: What's Your First Intuition?

Read the headline. Does it feel trustworthy? Does it seem like it might be false? Trust your first instinct. How does it make you feel?

Step 2: How Does It Make You Feel?

Does the news bring up strong emotions in you, like anger or excitement? Sometimes, news tries to use emotions to make us feel a certain way. Should we be suspicious when news tries to control our feelings?

Step 3: Does It Feel Exaggerated?

Look for clues. Does it sound too dramatic? News that uses extreme language can often be "clickbait" or fake. Statistics show that X% of exaggerated news stories are actually fake. What do you think about this one?

Step 4: Is There a Clear Message?

Does the news provide clear, objective information? Or is it trying to push an opinion? Sometimes, if there’s too much opinion, we need to look again.

Step 5: Is It Trying to Sell You Something?

Does the news make you want something? Maybe a product or lifestyle? Be cautious—news that plays on our desires might be more of an ad than true information.
